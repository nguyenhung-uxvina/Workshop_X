## 1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Chapter 7 of "What Customers Want" presents the seventh step in the Outcome-Driven Innovation (ODI) process: **systematically prioritizing development pipeline projects based on their ability to address underserved customer outcomes**. This methodology replaces subjective decision-making with data-driven prioritization, enabling companies to deliver winning products faster while reducing wasted development expense.

**Core Thesis:** Products and services that satisfactorily address targeted market opportunities (underserved customer outcomes) should receive high priority and accelerated resources. Those that fail to address these opportunities should be reconsidered or abandoned.

---

## 2. CLARIFICATION: Core Concepts Defined

### 2.1 The Opportunity Algorithm

The mathematical foundation for prioritization:

```
Opportunity = Importance + max(Importance - Satisfaction, 0)
```

**Where:**
- **Importance** = Percentage of customers rating outcome 4 or 5 on 5-point scale (converted to 10-point scale)
- **Satisfaction** = Percentage of customers rating current solutions 4 or 5 on 5-point scale (converted to 10-point scale)

**Interpretation Scale:**
| Score Range | Category | Action |
|-------------|----------|--------|
| >14 | Critical opportunity | Highest priority - accelerate |
| 12-14 | High opportunity | Strong priority - fund |
| 10-12 | Moderate opportunity | Second-tier - monitor |
| <10 | Low opportunity | Consider reducing/abandoning |

### 2.2 Key Terms Defined

| Term | Definition | Defense Training Context |
|------|------------|-------------------------|
| **Underserved Outcome** | Important outcome not well satisfied by current solutions | Training task customers struggle with using current systems |
| **Overserved Outcome** | Satisfied beyond importance level - customers won't pay more | Training capability already excellent - no value in improving |
| **Pipeline Initiative** | Product/feature/concept currently funded but not launched | VN-xxx products in various development stages |
| **Targeted Outcomes** | Prioritized list of underserved outcomes for improvement | Specific training gaps identified by defense customers |
| **Project Champion** | Individual with strong belief in project's success | Internal advocate for specific training system |

### 2.3 The Four Pipeline Prioritization Problems

Companies struggle with prioritization because they:

1. **Difficulty determining which concepts address market opportunities** → Root cause: Not knowing desired outcomes or which are underserved

2. **Compulsion to cover all bases** → Root cause: Uncertainty creates fear of missing competitor moves

3. **Difficulty killing projects once funded** → Root cause: Project champion exuberance and sunk cost fallacy

4. **Failure to allocate sufficient resources** → Root cause: Spreading resources across too many initiatives

```

        ↓ (+)

        ↓ (+)

        ↓ (+)

```
**Dominance:** HIGH - Currently driving system behavior

## 5. USE CASE RECOMMENDATIONS

### 5.1 Use Case 1: Quarterly Pipeline Review Meeting

**Situation:** Vietnam Defense holds quarterly review of 21 products in pipeline
**ODI Application:**

```
STEP 1: Preparation (2 weeks before)
├── Compile latest outcome research data
├── Update opportunity scores
└── Calculate each product's projected satisfaction impact

STEP 2: Evaluation Meeting (4 hours)
├── Review targeted outcomes (top 10 underserved)
├── Each product team presents satisfaction estimates
├── Cross-functional team validates estimates
└── Generate priority ranking

STEP 3: Resource Allocation Decision
├── Top 5 products: Full resource allocation
├── Middle 8 products: Monitored continuation
├── Bottom 8 products: Kill/defer decision
└── Document rationale with outcome data
```

**Expected Outcome:** Reduce pipeline from 21 to 13 active projects, concentrate resources, accelerate top performers.

### 5.2 Use Case 2: New Product Concept Screening

**Situation:** Engineering team proposes new "AR-VR Weapon Simulator" concept
**ODI Application:**

```
SCREENING QUESTIONS:
1. Which underserved outcomes does this concept address?
2. What estimated satisfaction score would it achieve for each?
3. Does total projected satisfaction exceed current solutions?
4. Is improvement focused on Opp Score >12 outcomes?

IF YES to all → Proceed to detailed evaluation
IF NO to any → Return for concept refinement or reject
```

**Template for New Concept Evaluation:**

| Criterion | Requirement | AR-VR Concept Score |
|-----------|-------------|---------------------|
| Addresses Outcome #1 (error identification) | Must improve | ✓ Est. 8.5 vs 3.8 |
| Addresses Outcome #2 (safety) | Must improve | ✓ Est. 9.0 vs 5.2 |
| Addresses Outcome #4 (stress realism) | Should improve | ✓ Est. 8.0 vs 4.8 |
| Does NOT reduce Outcome #2 (safety) | Must maintain | ✓ Improves |
| Total Opp Score impact | >40 | ✓ Est. 45.5 |

### 5.3 Use Case 3: Competitive Response Decision

**Situation:** Competitor launches new naval gunnery simulator
**ODI Application:**

```
DECISION TREE:

Does competitor's product address OUR targeted underserved outcomes?
├── YES: Does it address them better than our VN-NGS-001?
│   ├── YES: Accelerate VN-NGS-001 development
│   └── NO: Continue current pace
└── NO: Does competitor's product address overserved outcomes?
    ├── YES: Do NOT follow - they are wasting resources
    └── NO: Monitor but don't react
```

**Key Insight:** Don't assume competitor knows what they're doing. Evaluate their moves against YOUR outcome data.

### 5.4 Use Case 4: Feature vs. Product Decision

**Situation:** Team debates whether to add RAMS (Real-Time AI-Driven Marksmanship Training System) as feature to existing VN-SAMT-001 or as separate product
**ODI Analysis:**

**Feature Evaluation Logic:**
- Features typically address 1-2 outcomes well
- RAMS addresses: Error identification (Outcome #1), Assessment accuracy (#6)
- Both are high-opportunity outcomes

**Recommendation:** Add as feature to VN-SAMT-001 because:
1. RAMS improves satisfaction on 2 critical outcomes
2. Combined product addresses 6 of top 7 outcomes
3. Faster time-to-market as feature vs. new product
4. Lower development risk

## 9. KEY TAKEAWAYS

### 9.1 For Product Managers

1. **Stop covering all bases** - Certainty comes from outcome data, not breadth of bets
2. **Use the algorithm** - Opportunity = Importance + max(Importance - Satisfaction, 0)
3. **Evaluate against targeted outcomes** - Not against competitor products
4. **Features can be powerful** - Even single-outcome impact justifies funding if outcome is critical
5. **Kill with confidence** - Objective data defeats project champion exuberance

### 9.2 For Engineers

1. **Know your customer's metrics** - Desired outcomes are the specifications that matter
2. **Design for underserved outcomes** - Not for interesting technical challenges
3. **Measure satisfaction impact** - "What percentage would rate this 4 or 5?"
4. **Combine features strategically** - Add features that address different high-opportunity outcomes

### 9.3 For Executives

1. **Reduce pipeline, increase focus** - Better to fund 10 projects well than 50 poorly
2. **Change the goal** - From "don't miss opportunities" to "deliver highest value fastest"
3. **Reward outcome identification** - Employees who find underserved outcomes create value
4. **Break the champion loop** - Objective evaluation data breaks subjective belief

### 9.4 For Vietnam Defense Industry

1. **VN-SAMT-001 is your stent** - Like Cordis, identify and accelerate your breakthrough
2. **Training effectiveness outcomes are underserved** - Not training infrastructure
3. **LVC integration can wait** - High investment, moderate outcome impact
4. **Build outcome research capability** - The intelligence that drives all decisions
