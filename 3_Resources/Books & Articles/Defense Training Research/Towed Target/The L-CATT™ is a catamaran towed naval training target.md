## L-CATT™ Catamaran Towed Naval Training Target

The **L-CATT™ (Low-Cost Catamaran Tow Target)** is a specialized naval training platform engineered by QinetiQ Target Systems to serve military forces requiring realistic and cost-effective threat representation and weapons system evaluation capabilities.[^1]

### Physical Specifications and Construction

The L-CATT features a robust design optimized for marine environments and repeated operational deployments. The system measures **4.8 meters in length** and is constructed from **two rota-molded hulls of polyethylene**, providing a lightweight yet durable catamaran configuration. The rota-molded construction technique offers several inherent advantages, including seamless manufacturing, excellent durability and shock resistance, UV stabilization of materials, and inherent floatation characteristics that enhance survivability and mission reliability. This manufacturing approach produces components that are highly resistant to chemical degradation and capable of withstanding demanding maritime conditions across diverse sea states.[^2][^1]

### Operational Capabilities

The L-CATT operates as an **expendable target platform**, meaning it is specifically designed and economically justified for destruction during weapon systems testing and evaluation exercises. This expendable nature fundamentally distinguishes it from reusable training platforms and aligns with military training doctrine emphasizing realistic engagement scenarios without the operational constraints of platform preservation.[^1]

The catamaran can be **towed by multiple QinetiQ uncrewed surface vehicle platforms**, including the Sprite II, Hammerhead, and Barracuda, as well as conventional naval vessels and other existing maritime platforms. This broad compatibility enables integration into existing naval training infrastructure without requiring specialized deployment vessels. The Sprite II, measuring 4.2 to 7.5 meters in length, can operate at speeds exceeding 30 knots and is equipped with optional electric winches containing up to 500 meters of tow line to facilitate deployment and recovery operations.[^3][^1]

### Threat Representation and Payload Configuration

The L-CATT accommodates **a range of optional payloads** designed to replicate diverse naval threat profiles, enabling militaries to train against specific threat scenarios. The modular payload approach allows rapid reconfiguration between training exercises to represent various maritime adversary capabilities and vessel signatures. This payload flexibility extends the operational utility of the platform beyond simple surface targets to encompass complex threat representation scenarios including visual augmentation systems such as flags, flares, smoke, and strobes—payload options that enhance radar and visual detection training for defensive weapon systems.[^4][^1]

### Integration with Towing Platforms

The Hammerhead uncrewed surface vehicle, operating at speeds up to 35-40 knots depending on sea state, can be equipped with a towing capability allowing it to deploy and manage the L-CATT during training exercises. However, the Hammerhead is primarily employed as a reusable platform, with engagement focus directed toward towed targets to preserve the more expensive towing vessel. The Barracuda, a 7-meter fibreglass rigid inflatable boat powered by a 225 horsepower marine diesel engine and capable of 36 knots, functions as the primary "workhorse" platform for towing the L-CATT during extended training operations. The Barracuda's dual-role capability—serving as both a reusable platform and towing vessel—provides operational flexibility for navies balancing training requirements with asset preservation priorities.[^5][^6][^4]

### Operational Applications

The L-CATT supports comprehensive naval weapons system evaluation across multiple weapon categories:[^7]

- **Surface-to-Surface Missiles**: Harpoon, Exocet, RBS 15, and Mistral/SM/Sea Sparrow systems
- **Surface-to-Air Missiles** (in surface-to-surface mode): NATO Sea Sparrow, Evolved Sea Sparrow, Standard SM series
- **Air-to-Surface Missiles**: RBS 15, AGM-65 Maverick, CRV-7, Harpoon, Kormoran, LIDAM
- **Naval Guns and Close-In Weapon Systems**: Phalanx, Bushmaster (25-30mm), 40mm and 57mm Bofors, 76mm Oto Melara, 100mm Creusot Loire, 4.5-inch Royal Ordnance, and 127mm systems


### Distinctive Operational Advantages

The L-CATT design emphasizes **low-cost operations** aligned with modern military budget constraints and the requirement for extended training frequency without proportional cost escalation. The easily towable configuration minimizes operational complexity and training overhead, allowing crews to focus on weapons system proficiency rather than platform management. The **proven worldwide service record** and established customer support infrastructure demonstrate operational maturity and reliability across diverse operational theaters and allied naval forces.[^8][^1]

### Strategic Training Context

Within the broader QinetiQ threat representation portfolio, the L-CATT complements more sophisticated platforms such as the Hammerhead and Banshee aerial targets in delivering comprehensive, multi-domain training scenarios. Modern naval forces face increasingly complex threat environments, including swarming tactics, anti-ship ballistic missiles, loitering munitions, and hypersonic threats, requiring realistic, cost-effective training platforms that can be deployed at scale without budgetary constraints limiting training frequency or fidelity. The L-CATT addresses this requirement by providing an expendable, easily deployed naval target that enables realistic surface warfare training while maintaining fiscal responsibility in military training operations.[^9][^8]
